Gigawatt — an AI compute campus in the high desert

Gigawatt is a 3D management sim about what it physically takes to serve the AI boom. You site buildings on a desert plateau — server halls, GPU pods, substations, solar fields, batteries, dry coolers and water-hungry chiller plants — and keep five things in balance: power, heat, water, compute and cash.

The sun matters: solar fields carry you by day and batteries bridge the night, while the desert heat derates your dry coolers exactly when your racks run hottest. Chillers cool harder but drink from a finite aquifer. Underfeed power or fall behind on cooling and machines throttle, overheat, and eventually fail dark.

Money arrives through a contract market — steady inference deals and high-stakes training runs with deadlines and penalties — priced off a booming spot market for compute. Runs are seeded: heat waves, dust storms, price surges and grid brownouts replay identically for a given seed. The goal: grow the campus to a full gigawatt of IT load before a bad week bankrupts you.
