Hyperscale — an AI compute campus in a river valley

Hyperscale is a 3D management sim about what it physically takes to serve the AI boom. You site buildings on a fenced campus at the edge of a river-valley town — server halls, GPU pods, substations, solar fields, wind turbines, batteries, gas peakers, dry coolers and water-hungry chiller plants — and keep power, heat, water, compute, cash and your neighbours in balance.

The sun matters: solar carries you by day, wind swings hour to hour, batteries bridge the night, and the summer heat derates your dry coolers exactly when your racks run hottest. Chillers cool harder but drink from a finite aquifer. Gas peakers run in any weather — and smear smog across the valley. Underfeed power or fall behind on cooling and machines throttle, overheat, and eventually fail dark.

There is a town at the end of your access road, and it is watching: smog, fan noise and aquifer drawdown push civic sentiment down; clean power pulls it up. Let it collapse and the county freezes your permits — or the residents blockade the road. Money arrives through a contract market — steady inference deals and high-stakes training runs with deadlines and penalties — priced off a booming spot market. Runs are seeded: heat waves, dust storms, price surges and brownouts replay identically for a given seed, and the whole campus hums, whirs and roars in procedural audio. The goal: a full gigawatt of IT load before a bad week bankrupts you.
