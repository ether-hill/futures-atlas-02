# The Woodchipper — research foundation (cited)

Source of truth for the "Swipe/Scenario the USAID cuts" project. Every figure is
sourced. **Modeled projections are NOT observed body counts** and must be labelled
as such; contested numbers are flagged. Two honest framings are preserved.

## Headline facts (observed)
- **EO 14169** (Jan 20 2025): 90-day foreign-aid pause. Jan 24: stop-work orders (Rubio cable).
- **Rubio, Mar 10 2025:** "83% of USAID programs" cancelled (~5,200 contracts). KFF's analysis of the official list: **5,341 of 6,200+ awards = 86%**, **$27.7B unobligated**. → headline: **~83–86% of awards terminated**.
- **USAID absorbed into State, Jul 1 2025**; ~94% of staff gone. IG fired Feb 11 (a day after warning of oversight collapse; apparently without the legally required 30-day notice).
- **Budget proportion:** USAID ≈ **$43.8B (FY2023 disbursements)** ≈ **0.6–0.7%** of the ~$6.8T federal budget. All US foreign aid = $71.9B = **1.2%**. Public *thinks* aid is ~26% of the budget (KFF poll); only 11% know it's ≤1%.
- **Cut value:** admin "value of canceled awards" ≈ **$54B USAID / ~$60B with State**; KFF "unobligated funds" on the terminated list = **$27.7B**. *Different measures — do not equate.*
- Sources: [White House EO](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/reevaluating-and-realigning-united-states-foreign-aid/) · [NPR Mar 10](https://www.npr.org/sections/goats-and-soda/2025/03/10/g-s1-52964/rubio-announces-that-83-of-usaid-contracts-will-be-canceled) · [KFF terminated list](https://www.kff.org/global-health-policy/the-usaid-list-of-terminated-global-health-awards-what-does-it-tell-us/) · [Pew](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/02/06/what-the-data-says-about-us-foreign-aid/) · [NPR Jul 1](https://www.npr.org/sections/goats-and-soda/2025/07/01/g-s1-75222/usaid-trump-humanitarian-rubio-musk)

## The anchor impact study (peer-reviewed, contested)
- **The Lancet** (Cavalcanti, Rasella et al.; online Jun 30 2025): cuts could cause **>14,051,750 additional deaths by 2030** (UI 8.5–19.7M), incl. **4,537,157 children <5**. USAID estimated to have *prevented* ~91.8M deaths 2001–2021. Poisson fixed-effects on 133 countries + microsimulation. DOI 10.1016/S0140-6736(25)01186-9. [fulltext](https://www.thelancet.com/article/S0140-6736(25)01186-9/fulltext) · [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40609560/)
- Contested both ways: a reproduction attempt says negative-binomial regression removes significance (no code released); a Lancet correspondence says it *under*-estimates. Cite as "most probable scenario," flagged.

## Lower-bound estimates (think tank)
- **CGD** (Kenny & Sandefur): FY2025 actual **500,000–1,000,000 lives lost** (outlays) / 670k–1.6M (obligations). US aid saves ~3.3M lives/yr baseline. Explicitly "guesswork." [CGD update](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/update-lives-lost-usaid-cuts) · [CGD baseline](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/how-many-lives-does-us-foreign-aid-save)
- **BU Impact Counter** (Brooke Nichols): live projection, **757,314 deaths at the 1-yr mark (Jan 20 2026)**, "majority children." Advocacy tracker built on peer-reviewed models (not independent corroboration). Disputed by HFAC, Rubio ("exaggerated"), Musk ("zero people have died"). [dashboard](https://www.impactcounter.com/dashboard) · [BU](https://www.bu.edu/sph/news/articles/2025/tracking-anticipated-deaths-from-usaid-funding-cuts/)

## Program-by-program (scale → cut → effect → projection)
- **PEPFAR/HIV** — 20.6M on ART; ~$6B/yr; 71% of 379 HIV awards cut. Observed: Mozambique −14% ART starts; KFF FY25Q4 PrEP initiation −41%. Modeled: ten Brink/WHO (Lancet HIV, peer-reviewed) **4.4–10.8M additional infections & 0.77–2.93M deaths by 2030**; UNAIDS 6.6M infections / 4.2M deaths by 2029. [KFF PEPFAR](https://www.kff.org/global-health-policy/the-trump-administrations-foreign-aid-review-status-of-pepfar/) · [WHO/Lancet HIV](https://www.who.int/news/item/26-03-2025-new-study-highlights-the-potential-impact-of-funding-cuts-on-the-hiv-response) · [UNAIDS](https://www.unaids.org/en/impact-US-funding-cuts/About)
- **Malaria (PMI)** — $777.7M (2023), 37% of global malaria financing; 80% of 157 awards cut. Observed (WaPo): ~$76M supplies undelivered; DRC Haut-Katanga >600 deaths H1 2025. Modeled (Malaria Atlas Project, peer-reviewed) **~14.9M extra cases & ~107,000 deaths in 2025** (1-yr freeze). [KFF PMI](https://www.kff.org/global-health-policy/the-trump-administrations-foreign-aid-review-status-of-the-presidents-malaria-initiative-pmi/) · [WaPo](https://www.washingtonpost.com/investigations/interactive/2025/usaid-trump-malaria-hiv-drugs-death/) · [MAP](https://malariaatlas.org/project-resources/pmi-2025/)
- **Tuberculosis** — largest bilateral TB donor; 79% of 162 awards cut. Observed: Uganda notifications −14% (WHO). Modeled (PLOS GPH/Stop TB, peer-reviewed) central **1.66M cases / 268,600 deaths**, worst case 10.68M/2.24M by 2030; pediatric (Harvard/BU) 2.5M child cases/340k deaths. [PLOS GPH](https://journals.plos.org/globalpublichealth/article?id=10.1371/journal.pgph.0004899) · [Harvard](https://hsph.harvard.edu/news/u-s-funding-cuts-could-result-in-nearly-9-million-child-tuberculosis-cases-1-5-million-child-deaths/)
- **Gavi/vaccines** — US ~13% of Gavi; RFK Jr. withdrew future funding Jun 25 2025. Modeled (Gavi) **75M children miss vaccination over 5 yrs → >1.2M child deaths**. [KFF Gavi](https://www.kff.org/global-health-policy/the-trump-administrations-foreign-aid-review-status-of-u-s-support-for-gavi-the-vaccine-alliance/)
- **Food for Peace / nutrition** — US = 46% of WFP funding. Observed: ~66,000 MT/$98M food stranded; ~500 MT biscuits (27,000 kids) set to be destroyed; ~5,000 t RUTF stuck at Edesia (484,000 children). Modeled: UNICEF >2.4M SAM children without RUTF in 2025; Lancet Glob Health MCH (Stover/Avenir, peer-reviewed) 140k maternal + 2.5M child deaths by 2030. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/7/16/usaid-food-for-nearly-30000-hungry-kids-to-be-destroyed-official) · [UNICEF](https://www.unicef.org/press-releases/least-14-million-children-face-disruptions-critical-nutrition-services-2025-unicef) · [Lancet GH MCH](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12447089/)
- **WASH** — water programs 254→99 (−69%). Observed (correlational, CGD/WHO): African cholera deaths +118% in 2025. [CGD cholera](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/cholera-deaths-nearly-doubled-africa-2025-cuts-aid-may-have-contributed)
- **Family planning** — 85% of 233 awards cut; ~$9.7M contraceptives slated for incineration in France. Modeled (Guttmacher) 17.1M unintended pregnancies / 34,000 maternal deaths in 1 yr. [Guttmacher](https://www.guttmacher.org/2025/02/just-numbers-impact-us-international-family-planning-assistance-2024) · [Reuters/CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/24/9point7-million-of-us-funded-contraceptives-for-poor-nations-to-be-burned-in-france-reuters.html)
- **Pandemic prep / Ebola** — 615 of 770 global-health awards (80%) cut. Musk said Ebola prevention "restored immediately"; AP found ≥4 of 5 Uganda Ebola contracts terminated. [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/goats-and-soda/2025/02/27/g-s1-50929/elon-musk-ebola-usaid)
- **Ukraine + refugees** — ~90% of Ukraine development aid suspended (~$1.4B, the single largest country cut); UNHCR cut ~3,500 posts. [CGD country](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/usaid-cuts-new-estimates-country-level)

## Claims vs fact-checks (the justification)
WaPo (Kessler, Feb 7): **of the White House's 12 marquee USAID waste examples, only 1 (the $1.5M Serbia DEI grant) was accurate**; the other 11 false/misleading/lacked context. FactCheck.org: of the cited "DEI/transgender" items, only Serbia was USAID-funded; the rest were State Dept.
- "$50M condoms to Gaza" → **FALSE** (no evidence; global condom spend ~$8.2M). [PolitiFact](https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2025/jan/30/karoline-leavitt/no-the-us-did-not-spend-50-million-to-fund-condoms/) Musk later: "Some of the things I say will be incorrect."
- "$8M to Politico" → **MISREPRESENTED** (USAID paid ~$44k; $8M was all-of-government). [FactCheck](https://www.factcheck.org/2025/02/trump-online-posts-misrepresent-government-subscriptions-to-news-services/)
- "$20M Sesame Street Iraq" → **MISLEADING** (early-childhood program "Ahlan Simsim"). "Transgender opera/comic/Ireland musical" → **NOT USAID (State Dept)**. [FactCheck](https://www.factcheck.org/2025/02/sorting-out-the-facts-on-waste-and-abuse-at-usaid/)
- Musk: "USAID is a criminal organization. Time for it to die." / "We spent the weekend feeding USAID into the wood chipper." (verified X quotes, Feb 2–3). [x](https://x.com/elonmusk/status/1886307316804263979)

## The real waste picture (the audit counterfactual)
- USAID was **PIIA-compliant FY2016–18, 2022, 2023**; clean ("unmodified") financial-statement audit, no material weaknesses; not among high-improper-payment agencies (govt-wide improper payments $186B/yr concentrated in Medicare/Medicaid/EITC/SNAP). FY2024 non-compliance was a *reporting* failure caused by the shutdown itself. [OIG FY2023](https://oig.usaid.gov/node/6841) · [OIG FY2024](https://oig.usaid.gov/node/7733) · [GAO $186B](https://www.gao.gov/blog/186-billion-was-lost-improper-payments-last-year-how-can-we-prevent-them-future)
- OIG questioned costs ~$8.6M–$39.5M per half-year (≈0.1–0.6% of funds audited); recoveries single-digit $M. Real cases: Chemonics $3.1M FCA settlement; $9M Syria diversion (prosecuted); a 20-yr bribery debarment. [OIG Spring 2025](https://oig.usaid.gov/sites/default/files/2025-05/Spring%202025%20Semiannual%20Report%20to%20Congress_0.pdf)
- Oversight already existed: independent IG, annual audits, grantee-audit program, suspension/debarment, partner vetting, GAO.

## Legality
- Appointments Clause — *Does v. Musk* (Judge Chuang, Mar 18): "No statute authorizes the Executive Branch to shut down USAID"; ordered systems restored. **4th Cir. stayed** it (Mar 25). [CBS](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/judge-finds-doges-usaid-shutdown-likely-unconstitutional/)
- Owed payments — Judge Ali ordered ~$2B released for completed work; **SCOTUS 5–4 (Mar 5) declined to block** (Roberts+Barrett joined liberals). Later D.C. Cir. narrowed; SCOTUS 6–3 (Sep 26) let a ~$4B pocket-rescission stand. [SCOTUSblog](https://www.scotusblog.com/2025/03/supreme-court-denies-trump-request-to-block-2-billion-foreign-aid-payment/)

## Reform counterfactual (audit, don't abolish)
Real problems experts agree on: ~89% of core accounts earmarked (CSIS); fragmentation across 20+ agencies; contractor concentration (Chemonics ~$1.5B/yr, only ~6% reaching local orgs); slow risk-averse procurement.
- **Natsios** (GWB R-admin): a review was "perfectly appropriate," but "Reform comes incrementally… course correct, not course destroy." "They destroyed the agency." [GBH](https://www.wgbh.org/news/local/2025-03-27/former-administrator-says-usaid-reforms-are-baloney)
- Proposals on the table: CGD "four practical steps" + results-based procurement; Brookings (cabinet rank, loosen earmarks, reward risk); Power's localization (25% local by 2025); MFAN bipartisan principles. [CGD](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/four-practical-steps-to-jump-start-foreign-assistance-reform) · [Brookings](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/aid-effectiveness-reform-in-the-new-administration-and-congress/)

## Two honest framings (build must present both)
1. **Reckless destruction:** the stated justifications were largely false (11/12 examples), documented waste was a fraction of 1%, a full audit apparatus already existed, no real audit preceded the cuts, the IG was fired, courts found the shutdown likely unlawful, and peer-reviewed models project millions of additional deaths with clinic-level disruption already observed.
2. **Real problems worth fixing:** USAID genuinely had heavy earmarking, fragmentation, contractor bloat (only ~6% to local orgs), and slow procurement; documented fraud was real (Chemonics, Syria); even Republican former administrators agreed a program review was legitimate and a serious reform agenda already existed — the objection is to abolition-by-sledgehammer, not to reform.

**Modeled, do not average / do not present as body counts:** Lancet 14M; CGD 0.5–1.6M; BU 757k; UNAIDS 4.2M; Gavi 1.2M child; PLOS TB 0.27–2.24M; Guttmacher 34k maternal. Always cite the scenario (freeze length, horizon, backfill).
